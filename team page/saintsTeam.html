WILL ALLMAN

  • D.O.B


    3 May 1997

  • BIRTHPLACE


    Derby (England)

  • Height


    185 cm

PLAYER BIOG Will Allman joined the Senior Academy in 2015 having impressed whilst at the Saints on a part-time basis during his A-Level studies at Trent College. His rugby career started when he was six years old and before joining the Saints he played for Melbourne RFC in Derby. During his time at school, Will helped Trent College win the NatWest Schools Vase at Twickenham in 2014 when they beat Exeter College, something he describes as his best rugby moment. Will comes from a strong rugby family, with his grandfather John captaining Sale in the 1960s. In 2015, Will was called up to play for England at Under-18 level in the European Championship, scoring against Portugal, and subsequently went on their tour to South Africa. Will is currently combining his rugby career with studying for a degree in Economics at Loughborough University. He says that his greatest ambition is to play full international rugby and that he most admires former All Black captain Richie McCaw. Away from the rugby pitch, Will’s favourite band are the Kings of Leon and his favourite film is Reservoir Dogs.

Born in Stoke-on-Trent, Kieran Brookes moved to the Saints for the 2015/16 season from Newcastle Falcons having been at the north east club for all but two seasons since turning professional in 2009. Sandwiched between his four seasons at Newcastle was a two-year stint at Leicester Tigers during which he played on loan for Doncaster Knights. A product of Kirkham Grammar School in Lancashire, Brookes spent 10 years playing for Fylde RFC in Lytham St Annes before joining Newcastle Falcons. Brookes was also educated in Perth, Australia and Grahamstown, South Africa, and represented Ireland at Under-18 and Under-20 levels, whilst also helping Lancashire win the Bill Beaumont Cup. Since making his full debut for England on the 2014 summer tour to New Zealand, Brookes has earned 16 caps for his country. He was a member England’s 2015 Rugby World Cup squad and was also involved with England’s Grand Slam success in the 2016 Six Nations. During the summer of 2016, Brookes played against South Africa 'A' on the England Saxons' summer tour, playing in both matches as the Saxons secured a 2-0 series victory.

Luther Burrell joined the Saints from Sale Sharks in 2012 and rapidly made an impression. A powerful centre, Luther was born in Huddersfield and represented his hometown club Huddersfield Giants in rugby league at age grade level before making the switch to rugby union. His professional rugby union career began at Leeds Carnegie in 2009 before he joined Sale Sharks in 2011. Burrell first represented England in a non-cap match against the CONSUR XV on England’s tour to Argentina in the summer of 2013. He was then called up to the England squad for the 2014 Six Nations after impressive performances for the Saints and made an immediate impression. He scored a try for England on his international debut against France and subsequently scored against Scotland and Wales during that same Championship. His 2014 season was capped off with a place on England’s summer tour to New Zealand. Burrell has earned 15 England caps so far and was a key member of the Saints squad that won the Aviva Premiership in 2014. In May 2016, Luther was recalled to the England squad for their summer Test against Wales at Twickenham. Following his try-scoring antics against Wales, Burrell travelled to Australia for England's summer tour, playing in the first Test in Brisbane as England secured an historic 3-0 Test series win over the Wallabies.

Born in Blackpool, Christian Day was educated at Arnold School before joining Sale Sharks in 2003 at the age of 20. Before joining the Saints in 2008, Day had spent the entirety of his professional career with the north west club, apart from a short spell at French side Stade Français during the 2007/08 season. Since joining the Saints, Day has been a vital member of the pack, proving incredibly influential at the set piece. In 2014 Day was involved in both of the Saints’ cup-winning finals having also played in the European Challenge Cup final in 2009, and the finals of the 2011 Heineken Cup and 2012 LV=Cup. Along with his Saints team mate Ben Foden, his participation in the 2014 Premiership final was the second time Day had won the Premiership. He was a substitute for Sale, alongside Ben, as they beat Leicester in 2006. Day's efforts as a member of the double-winning squad in 2013/14 were rewarded as he was named the Players’ Player of the Season. At international level, Day has represented England Schools, England Under-19s and England Saxons and he also captained the England Under-21s. Outside of his playing career Day is the chairman of the Rugby Players’ Association, a role he has held since 2014.

Bedford-born Jamie Elliott made his debut for the Saints in the LV= Cup in 2011 whilst only 18 years old. BEN FODEN

  • D.O.B


    22 July 1985

  • BIRTHPLACE


    Chester
    (England)

  • Height


    183 cm

Whilst his showbiz status has grabbed plenty of column inches in recent years, it is testament to Ben Foden’s focus that when he steps onto a rugby pitch he remains one of Europe’s most potent counter-attacking full backs. Educated at Bishop Heber High School and Bromsgrove School, Foden's professional career started at Sale Sharks in 2004, and he was a replacement in Sale Sharks’ 2006 Premiership final victory over Leicester Tigers. He joined the Saints in 2008 with aspirations of establishing himself at scrum half. However, Foden subsequently impressed at full back, with a string of impressive performances in the 15 jersey. As a result, it is not known if Philippe Saint-André, Foden's coach at Sale Sharks, stuck to his words and ate his French beret! Foden has since become a regular at full back in the Saints starting XV. In his first season at the Saints, the full back helped the club win the 2009 European Challenge Cup and played a major role in the double cup-winning side of 2014, scoring a try in the Aviva Premiership final against Saracens. In doing so, he became only the second player after Lewis Moody to score in two consecutive Premiership finals. At international level Foden represented England at Under-16s, Under-19s and Under-21s before representing the England Saxons at the 2008 Churchill Cup. Foden made his full England debut as a replacement scrum half in the 2009 Six Nations against Italy at Twickenham before playing in a non-capped match against the Barbarians in May 2009. In 2010 he became a regular in the England side and was part of England’s squad at the 2011 Rugby World Cup, scoring tries against Romania and France. In total, Foden has played 34 Tests for England, scoring seven tries. Interestingly, Foden has played against France four times during his international career, scoring a try on each occasion. Hitting his 200th cap for Saints in the 2016/17 season, Foden has become somewhat of a stalwart of the club though has recently been pushing his younger opposition for a spot on the wing.

Gibson has marked himself out as one of the Aviva Premiership’s players to watch in the last few years. Born in London, Jamie attended Marlborough College in Wiltshire before studying Classics at University College, Oxford where injuries twice prevented him from playing in the annual Varsity match. Gibson's professional rugby career began at London Irish in 2009 during which time he received his first call up to the senior England squad for their summer tour to South Africa in 2012. Jamie played in both matches against the SA Barbarians. Gibson spent five seasons playing at the Berkshire club before moving to Leicester Tigers in 2013, where he became a regular in the side almost immediately. Gibson made an immediate impression when he joined the Saints in 2015, with strong performances seeing him win the Saints’ Player of the Month award for October 2015. In 2015/16 he played a total of 1435 minutes of Premiership rugby, third only to Luther Burrell and Ben Foden. Prior to his call up to the full England squad in 2012, Gibson had won the Grand Slam with England Under-18s in 2008 before representing England at Under-20 level at the 2010 Junior World Championship. In 2011, Gibson was a member of the England Saxons squad that won the Churchill Cup and he was also named in the squad again in January 2012. He played for England in the 2014 non-cap match against the Barbarians at Twickenham.

The 2015/16 season was undoubtedly the best season yet for Teimana Harrison. New Zealand-born Harrison became a regular starter for Jim Mallinder’s side, with his impressive performances also earning him a call up to Eddie Jones’ squad for England’s summer tour to Australia. The powerful number eight’s impressive season culminated in him scooping Breakthrough Player, Supporters’ Player and Players’ Player at the Saints' End of Season awards in addition to two Steffans Player of the Month awards and an Aviva Premiership Player of the Month award. Born in Opotiki on New Zealand’s North Island, Harrison was scouted by Dylan Hartley when the England hooker visited his old school, Rotorua Boys’ High School, during the 2011 Rugby World Cup. Harrison also captained Rotorua Boys’ High School, with his headmaster once describing his style of play as “mongrel”. One of New Zealand’s leading rugby schools, alumni include All Blacks Liam Messam and Craig Newby. Harrison first joined the Saints as a member of the Senior Academy in late 2011 before making the transition into the first team in 2012. His debut for the first team came against Wasps later that year. Hartley’s inkling that Harrison was a special player proved correct and since his arrival in the East Midlands the dogged and tenacious back row has become one of the Premiership’s most dangerous ball carriers. In May 2016, Harrison received his first call up to the England Senior side, making his debut against Wales at Twickenham. He was subsequently named in England's squad to tour Australia, starting the third and final Test in Sydney as England won their first ever Test series on Australian soil. Harrison was also a part of the England squad for the 2017 Six Nations and helped the country become champions for the second year running alongside fellow Saints Courtney Lawes, Dylan Hartley and Tom Wood.

Dylan Hartley is regarded as one of the finest exponents of hooker play in the country, and for good reason. Not only was he captain of the Saints during a successful five-year period, but he spearheaded a pack that destroyed opponents in the Aviva Premiership and European Cup. Born in Rotorua, New Zealand, Hartley moved to Crowborough, East Sussex in 2002 where he played rugby for local teams. Hartley's professional rugby career began when he joined the Worcester Warriors academy before joining the Saints Senior Academy in the summer of 2005. During his time at the Saints Hartley has helped his club return to the Premiership, and captained the club to the 2011 Heineken Cup final and 2013 Aviva Premiership final. The following year saw the Saints win both the Aviva Premiership and European Challenge Cup with Hartley club captain once again as the Saints were victorious in both finals. Hartley's performances for the Saints have also led to him earning considerable international experience too. In 2005 he represented England at the Under-21 Rugby World Championship in Argentina. In 2007 Hartley made his debut for the England Saxons before receiving his first cap for England the following year against the Pacific Islanders at Twickenham. His first Test start came at Old Trafford in 2009 when England played Argentina. Hartley was a member of the England squad for the 2011 Rugby World Cup, having also been a part of the England side that won the Six Nations earlier that year. In January 2016 Hartley was appointed England captain by new head coach Eddie Jones and just two months later he led England to a first Grand Slam in 13 years after England beat France in Paris. After a successful Six Nations campaign Hartley went on to lead England to an historic Test series victory over Australia. England won all three Tests against the Wallabies and their first ever series win on Australian soil. Hartley captained England to a successive Six Nations title in 2017, alongside fellow Saints Courtney Lawes, Teimana Harrison and Tom Wood, and is currently leading a young squad out in Argentina.

Local boy Courtney Lawes was raised just a stone's throw from Franklin's Gardens and has progressed to a level where he is now indispensable to the Saints. He has also proved invaluable to England too having taken responsibility of running his country’s lineout. Lawes was a member of the Saints Academy and made his first team debut in 2007 against Esher in National One. After the Saints were promoted back to the Premiership, Lawes continued to impress during the 2008/09 season in both the Guinness Premiership and Challenge Cup, receiving the Man of the Match award in the home game against Montpellier. Later that season Lawes came on as a replacement to help the Saints win the Challenge Cup final against Bourgoin, before being involved in the Saints’ LV=Cup victory in 2010. Since breaking into the Saints first team, Lawes has continually impressed with his work rate and ability to put in some huge hits. This aspect of his game has played a big role in helping him to gain international honours at various levels. Having represented England at Under-20 level during the 2009 IRB Junior World Championship, Lawes was selected in the England Saxons squad. Senior England honours came later that year when he made his debut as a replacement in the autumn international against Australia at Twickenham. Since then, he has gone on to represent England at two Rugby World Cups, won the Six Nations in 2011 and helped England to their first Grand Slam in 13 years during the 2016 Six Nations. He was also called up to England's squad for their 2016 summer tour to Australia, helping England to an historic 3-0 Test series win over the Wallabies. More recently Lawes helped England win their second consecutive Six Nations in 2017 before earning the call-up to the British and Irish Lions over the summer. Lawes has since earned two Lions caps, impressing greatly in both games.

Born in Kettering, prop Alex Waller wrote his name into the Saints history books with his extra time try against Saracens in 2014 to give his side a first Premiership title. A product of the Saints Academy, Waller was educated at Wellingborough School and made his Saints debut in November 2009 against Ospreys. Young Player of the season in 2012, Waller is the only player to appear in 100 consecutive Premiership games having reached the landmark against Bath during 2015/16. In total, Waller has played in 146 consecutive Premiership and European matches, stretching back to the 2011 Heineken Cup final and including Saints’ cup double in 2014. Waller has also played at international level, making his debut for the England Saxons against Irish Wolfhounds and earning his second cap the following week against Scotland A. This call up was his first inclusion in any of England’s representative sides. Following his match-winning antics in the Premiership final, Waller joined up with the senior England squad for their three-Test tour of New Zealand that summer. He played against the Crusaders alongside fellow Saints Ben Foden, Lee Dickson and Stephen Myler. Waller was also a member of the England XV side that played against the Barbarians at Twickenham in May 2015. Most recently Waller secured the all time record for the most number of consecutive starts in the Aviva Premiership, having started at loos head in 140 consecutive league games.

The past 12 months have seen Harry Mallinder go from relative unknown to a star of the Saints first team. Since making the move into full-time rugby after completing his studies at Rugby School, where he captained the first team, Mallinder has proved himself to be a real talent across the Saints back line. A product of the Saints Academy, Sale-born Mallinder made his first team debut during the 2014/15 season with his breakthrough coming during the 2015/16 campaign. His 78th minute try against Glasgow Warriors at Franklin’s Gardens in the European Champions Cup secured a late 19-15 victory for the Saints and since then he has become a regular in the starting XV at either centre or full back. Mallinder has also impressed on the international scene too, making his England Under-18 debut at centre against Scotland in March 2014, scoring two penalties and a conversion in a 28-6 victory. Mallinder captained England Under-18s to victory over Portugal in the FIRA/AER Championship the following month before helping his side take the European crown with victory over Ireland in the final. In May 2016, Mallinder was named in the England squad for the World Rugby Under-20 Championship taking place in Manchester. The fly-half went on to captain England from the pool stages through to the final, as England were crowned World Champions. In the final against Ireland Mallinder scored 23 points, including two tries, in a man-of-the-match performance. Becoming a solid contender in the first team at centre over the 2016/17 season, Mallinder showed his maturity throughout the season but most notably as he kicked over the winning conversion to secure Saints European Champions Cup rugby for the 2017/18 season against State Francais. Mallinder's performances earned him England recognition and the young back is currently in Argentina with the national side for their summer tour, alongside fellow Saints Dylan Hartley, Piers Francis and Jamal Ford-Robinson.

GEORGE NORTH

  • D.O.B


    13 April 1992

  • BIRTHPLACE

    (England)

Despite only being 24 years old, George North has established himself as one of the world’s best wingers. King's Lynn-born North moved to Wales at the age of two and marked his Test debut for Wales by scoring two tries in a narrow 29-25 defeat to then World Champions South Africa in Cardiff. These tries made him the youngest Welshman to score on his debut. North's professional career started with Llandovery in 2010, a feeder club for Scarlets whom he joined 2010. During his three years with Scarlets, North scored 14 tries for the West Wales region before joining the Saints in the summer of 2013. His first season at the Saints ended with him helping the side win both the European Challenge Cup and the Aviva Premiership scoring seven tries in 15 Premiership appearances. Since exploding onto the international scene in 2010, North has amassed 60 caps, cementing his place in the starting XV. In addition to this, he has won three caps for the British and Irish Lions, scoring crucial tries in the first and third Tests as the Lions earned an historic Test series victory in 2013. North has broken multiple records. He became the first teenager to reach 10 Test tries, and had played 21 times for Wales before turning 20. His tries at the 2011 Rugby World Cup made him the youngest try scorer at a World Cup as Wales reached the semi-finals for the first time in over 20 years. Post-World Cup, North played a crucial role as Wales won the Grand Slam in 2012 and defended their title the following year. A regular in the Wales side, North also represented his country at the 2015 Rugby World Cup with Wales making the quarter finals. He then went on to become top scorer in the 2016 Six Nations with four tries and was subsequently selected for Wales’ summer tour to New Zealand. Featuring in Wales' Six Nations squad for the 2017 campaign, North's performances in the Aviva Premiership earned him the call-up to the British and Irish Lions squad for their New Zealand tour, making his first start against the Crusaders in a warm-up game.

Coventry-born Tom Wood made an immediate impression when he joined the Saints in 2010, with a man-of-the-match performance against rivals Leicester Tigers. Educated at Woodland’s School in Coventry, Wood's professional rugby career began at North Otago in 2006 whilst spending time in New Zealand. On his return to England, Tom signed for Worcester Warriors and went on to help his side reach the 2008 final of the European Challenge Cup. Wood joined the Saints for the 2010/11 season and his first campaign with the club saw him named Aviva Premiership Player of the Season. Since arriving at the Saints, Wood has proved to be a vital part of the Saints pack with a relentless work rate that has been crucial in the past few seasons. He was a starter in the 2013 Aviva Premiership final against Leicester and scored the crucial try in the semi finals the following season, also against Leicester, to put the Saints one step closer to their first ever domestic title. In the final, Wood captained the side and lifted the trophy alongside Club captain Dylan Hartley as the Saints secured a Cup double having already won the Challenge Cup. Wood has also had a successful international career making his debut for England against Wales in Cardiff at the start of the 2011 Six Nations with England winning their first Six Nations title since 2003. Later that year he was named in England’s squad for the 2011 Rugby World Cup in New Zealand. Wood returned to the international scene during the November internationals, putting in a man-of-the-match performance as England recorded a 38-21 victory over World Champions New Zealand. Captaining England during their 2013 tour to Argentina, Wood played in his second Rugby World Cup in 2015. Wood returned to England duties in 2017, becoming a part of the squad for the Six Nations campaign and helping the national side to their second consecutive title alongside fellow Saints Courtney Lawes, Teimana Harrison and captain Dylan Hartley.
